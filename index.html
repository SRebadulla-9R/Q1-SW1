What is Volleyball?

Volleyball is a sport involving a ball that is usually colored yellow & blue or plain white. It is a game where there are two teams on both sides of the court that is divided by a net. A game consists of two teams with six players and it usually has 2-3 sets. Players must pass the ball using their forearms or their hands. Before the game starts one player has to serve the ball to the other side. Both teams must avoid the ball from hitting the floor or the opposing team will get a point.

What are the positions and zones in the court in volleyball?

The line up starts at the very back, where number 1 starts the game with his or her service. All players rotate in a clockwise order after each point their team wins. (The following zones and names will be shown under.)

    • Opposite hitter - is positioned on the right side of the court, opposite from the setter.
    • Middle blocker - player that prevents an attack from the opposing team.
    • Outside hitter - is positioned on the left side of the court.
    • Libero - the player that prevents the ball from hitting the ground by diving or recieving
